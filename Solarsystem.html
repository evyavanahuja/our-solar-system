Our Solar System

An overview of the eight major planets orbiting our Sun

Mercury Venus Earth Mars Jupiter Saturn Uranus Neptune

The Inner Planets (Terrestrial)

Mercury

Terrestrial Planet

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It experiences extreme temperature swings from scorching hot days to freezing nights due to its lack of atmosphere.

  • Distance from Sun: 0.39 AU
  • Moons: 0
  • Year Length: 88 Earth days

Venus

Terrestrial Planet

Earth's twin in size, but covered in a thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide. This traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

  • Distance from Sun: 0.72 AU
  • Moons: 0
  • Year Length: 225 Earth days

Earth

Terrestrial Planet

Our home planet and the only known world capable of supporting life. Its surface is unique for containing liquid water and a vibrant atmosphere.

  • Distance from Sun: 1.00 AU
  • Moons: 1
  • Year Length: 365.25 days

Mars

Terrestrial Planet

Known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It features massive volcanoes, deep canyons, and thin atmospheric layers.

  • Distance from Sun: 1.52 AU
  • Moons: 2
  • Year Length: 687 Earth days

The Outer Planets (Giants)

Jupiter

Gas Giant

The largest planet in our solar system. It is a massive ball of hydrogen and helium, famous for its Great Red Spot—a storm larger than Earth raging for centuries.

  • Distance from Sun: 5.20 AU
  • Moons: 95 confirmed
  • Year Length: 12 Earth years

Saturn

Gas Giant

The second-largest planet, world-renowned for its extensive, spectacular ring system made of billions of ice particles, rocky debris, and dust.

  • Distance from Sun: 9.58 AU
  • Moons: 146 confirmed
  • Year Length: 29 Earth years

Uranus

Ice Giant

An ice giant with a unique pale cyan hue caused by atmospheric methane. It is highly unusual because it rotates completely on its side.

  • Distance from Sun: 19.22 AU
  • Moons: 28 confirmed
  • Year Length: 84 Earth years

Neptune

Ice Giant

The most distant planet from our Sun. It is a deep blue, freezing ice giant that experiences supersonic winds, the strongest recorded in the solar system.

  • Distance from Sun: 30.05 AU
  • Moons: 16 confirmed
  • Year Length: 165 Earth years
